Data Is Always Someone's Story

Building dashboards for teachers in Paris taught me that numbers aren't neutral. A student's engagement score represents late nights studying, family pressure, or genuine curiosity. When I clean messy data, I'm not just fixing inconsistencies‚ÄîI'm making someone's reality more visible.

Building vs. Understanding

I used to think success meant automating everything. Now I realize some friction is useful. When educators manually checked student records, they caught patterns algorithms missed‚Äîthe quiet kid who stopped participating, the high achiever burning out. Sometimes the inefficient way teaches you what efficiency erases.

Cultural Code-Switching

Working across Shanghai, Cardiff, and Paris revealed that problem-solving has accents. British teams wanted documentation before acting. Chinese colleagues preferred iterative testing. French stakeholders valued elegant solutions over quick fixes. I learned to adapt not just languages, but collaboration styles.
